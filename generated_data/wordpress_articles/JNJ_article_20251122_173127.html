Johnson & Johnson (JNJ) Company Description

Johnson & Johnson, founded in 1886 and headquartered in New Brunswick, New Jersey, is a global powerhouse in the healthcare field. The company is actively involved in the research and development, manufacturing, and sale of a vast array of products worldwide. It operates primarily through two key segments: Innovative Medicine and MedTech. The Innovative Medicine segment focuses on developing products across various therapeutic areas. This includes immunology, targeting conditions like rheumatoid arthritis and psoriasis; infectious diseases, such as HIV/AIDS; and neuroscience, addressing mood disorders and schizophrenia. Furthermore, this segment is active in oncology, cardiovascular and metabolism, and pulmonary hypertension, serving patients through a wide network of retailers, wholesalers, hospitals, and healthcare professionals. Meanwhile, the MedTech segment provides advanced medical technologies. This encompasses electrophysiology products for heart rhythm disorders, and a heart recovery portfolio for severe coronary artery disease. It also includes circulatory restoration products for calcified coronary artery and peripheral artery diseases, and neurovascular care for stroke treatments. Additionally, MedTech offers an extensive orthopaedics portfolio (hips, knees, trauma, spine) and advanced surgery technologies. It's also known for its ACUVUE contact lenses and TECNIS intraocular lenses for cataract surgery. The MedTech products are distributed to hospitals, retailers, and directly to physicians, nurses, and eye care professionals.
