Albemarle Corporation (ALB) Company Profile & Business Overview

To truly understand a stock, we need to know the company behind it. Albemarle Corporation (ALB) is a global leader specializing in energy storage solutions, operating across three key segments: Energy Storage, Specialties, and Ketjen.

Albemarle Corporation Business Description

Albemarle is at the forefront of providing essential materials for a modern, electrified world. Their Energy Storage segment is particularly critical, offering various lithium compounds like lithium carbonate, lithium hydroxide, and lithium chloride. These are indispensable for lithium batteries used in everything from consumer electronics to the booming electric vehicle market, as well as power grids and solar panels.

Beyond batteries, this segment supports high-performance greases, specialty glass, and plays a role in organic synthesis processes for steroid chemistry, vitamins, and other life science applications. They also provide technical services for reactive lithium products and recycling services for lithium-containing by-products, underscoring their comprehensive approach to the lithium value chain.

The Specialties segment broadens Albemarle's reach with bromine and highly specialized lithium solutions. These cater to diverse industries such as energy, mobility, connectivity, and health. Products include fire safety compounds, a wide array of bromine-based specialty chemicals, and lithium specialties like butyllithium. They also supply cesium products for the chemical and pharmaceutical industries, and zirconium, barium, and titanium for pyrotechnical applications, including airbag initiators.

Finally, the Ketjen segment offers advanced clean fuels technologies, including hydroprocessing catalysts, isomerization, and alkylation catalysts. This segment also provides fluidized catalytic cracking catalysts and additives, along with performance catalyst solutions such as organometallics and curatives. Ketjen serves a broad client base, from grid storage and automotive to aerospace, conventional energy, electronics, construction, agriculture, food, pharmaceuticals, and medical device industries. Founded in 1887, Albemarle Corporation is headquartered in Charlotte, North Carolina.
