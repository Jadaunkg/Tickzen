Business Overview

Pfizer Inc. is a global biopharmaceutical powerhouse. The company is actively involved in discovering, developing, manufacturing, marketing, distributing, and selling a wide range of biopharmaceutical products across the United States and internationally. It's a massive operation with a significant impact on global health.

Their portfolio is incredibly diverse, covering various therapeutic areas. For cardiovascular and migraine, they offer well-known brands like Eliquis, Nurtec ODT/Vydura, Zavzpret, and the Premarin family. In infectious diseases, they address unmet medical needs with products such as the Prevnar family, Abrysvo, Nimenrix, FSME/IMMUN-TicoVac, and Trumenba.

Of course, we can't forget their significant contributions to COVID-19 prevention and treatment with Comirnaty and Paxlovid, alongside potential future mRNA and antiviral products. Pfizer also provides biosimilars for chronic immune and inflammatory diseases (Xeljanz, Enbrel, Inflectra, Litfulo, Velsipity, Cibinqo), and medicines for amyloidosis, hemophilia, endocrine diseases, and sickle cell disease (Vyndaqel family, Oxbryta, BeneFIX, Somavert, Ngenla, Genotropin).

Their sterile injectable and anti-infective medicines include Sulperazon, Medrol, Zavicefta, Zithromax, Octagam, and Panzyga. Furthermore, Pfizer is deeply involved in biologics, small molecules, immunotherapies, and biosimilars under brands like Ibrance, Xtandi, Padcev, Adcetris, Inlyta, Lorbrena, Bosulif, Tukysa, Braftovi, Mektovi, Orgovyx, Elrexfio, Tivdak, and Talzenna. Beyond drug development, the company also engages in contract manufacturing.

Pfizer serves a broad spectrum of clients, including wholesalers, retailers, hospitals, clinics, government agencies, pharmacies, individual provider offices, and integrated delivery systems. They also have notable collaboration agreements with industry giants like Bristol-Myers Squibb Company, Astellas Pharma US, Inc., Merck KGaA, and BioNTech SE. Founded in 1849, Pfizer Inc. remains headquartered in New York, New York, a testament to its enduring legacy in the pharmaceutical world.
