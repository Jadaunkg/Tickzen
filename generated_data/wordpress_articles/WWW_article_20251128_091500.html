Wolverine World Wide's Core Business & Brands

To truly understand WWW's potential, we need to grasp its core business. Wolverine World Wide, Inc. is a global company involved in the design, manufacture, sourcing, marketing, licensing, and distribution of footwear, apparel, and accessories. Their reach extends across the United States, Europe, the Middle East, Africa, Asia Pacific, Canada, and Latin America, operating primarily through its Active Group and Work Group segments.

The company offers a diverse range of products, including casual footwear and apparel, performance outdoor and athletic footwear and apparel, kids' footwear, industrial work boots and apparel, and uniform shoes and boots. They manage an impressive portfolio of well-known brands such as Bates, Cat, Chaco, Harley-Davidson, Hush Puppies, Hytest, Merrell, Saucony, Sperry, Keds, Sweaty Betty, and Wolverine. Additionally, they license the Stride Rite brand.

Wolverine World Wide also markets Merrell and Wolverine branded apparel and accessories, and licenses its brands for non-footwear products, including Hush Puppies apparel, eyewear, watches, socks, handbags, and plush toys, as well as Wolverine branded eyewear and gloves. Their Wolverine Leather division markets pigskin leather, and a dedicated sourcing division provides crucial consulting services for product development, production control, quality assurance, and materials procurement.

The company's multi-brand direct-to-consumer division includes both retail stores and e-commerce sites, selling footwear and apparel from its extensive brand portfolio. They distribute products to a wide array of customers, including department stores, national chains, catalog and specialty retailers, independent retailers, uniform outlets, and mass merchant and government customers. Founded in 1883, Wolverine World Wide is headquartered in Rockford, Michigan. You can learn more about their operations and investor relations at the Wolverine World Wide Investor Relations page.
