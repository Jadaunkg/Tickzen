FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about StockAnalyzer Pro's features and capabilities

How accurate are the technical indicators and forecasts?
Our AI models analyze 15 years of historical data using advanced algorithms for RSI, MACD, and Histogram calculations. The forecast tables include confidence intervals and are based on proven technical patterns. While no analysis can predict markets with 100% accuracy, our users report 73% improvement in their investment timing decisions.
How does the WordPress automation prevent spam detection?
Our smart scheduling system allows you to set minimum and maximum posting intervals (e.g., 2-6 hours) with randomized timing. The writer rotation system automatically cycles through different author profiles, creating natural publishing patterns that search engines and hosting providers recognize as legitimate content.
Can I customize which sections appear in my WordPress posts?
Yes! You have complete control over all 12 available sections including introduction, technical analysis, fundamental analysis, forecast, risk factors, and more. You can create different configurations for different sites and customize the content depth for each section.
What data sources do you use for the 15-year historical analysis?
We aggregate data from premium financial sources including Bloomberg, Reuters, Yahoo Finance, SEC filings, and major exchanges. Our historical price tables and charts cover 15 years of data with daily, weekly, and monthly granularity for comprehensive trend analysis.
How does the SEO optimization work?
Every generated report includes stock ticker keyword optimization, 1-2 strategic external links to authoritative financial sources, proper meta descriptions, FAQ sections, and schema markup. The content is structured with H2/H3 headers and optimized for financial search terms to maximize organic visibility.
What's included in the upcoming historical events analyzer?
This feature will analyze how stocks reacted to major historical events (earnings, splits, market crashes, etc.) and identify patterns in technical indicators during those periods. It will help predict how similar events might affect stock prices based on historical precedent and current market conditions.
